whoami
I started programming at age 15. Not uncommon for someone born in Palo Alto, but given my Slavic roots and binary birthday (thx y2k), I like to think that it was coded into my DNA. Although a lot has changed over the last 9 years, my love for code stays rooted in the beauty of the binary. It either works, or it doesn't. With code, success is (for the most part) all-or-nothing, leaving no room for subjective reasoning and misinterpretation. A merciless critic, unforgiving of typos and shortcuts. With no indication of progress the writing process sometimes feels like purgatory, but! Upon success, there is no ambiguity in your accomplishment. I love that. Of course there are exceptions and nuances to this line of reasoning, but this concept endears me nonetheless.
